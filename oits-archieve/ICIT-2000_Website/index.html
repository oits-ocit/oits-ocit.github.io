Call For Participation

 
The International Conference on Information Technology (CIT 2000) will be held at Bhubaneswar, India, during 21-23 December, 2000 CIT has established itself as a successful annual international conference in Information Technology in India. It provides a high quality forum for scientists and engineers to present their latest research findings in this rapidly changing field. The earlier conference CIT'98 and CIT'99 were held in Bhubaneswar in December with active participation of academia and IT Industries. Participants from 10 different countries in North America, Europe, Asia and Australia have attended the conference. The conference arranges invited talks, special sessions and tutorials on cutting edge topics in Information Technology. CIT’98 and CIT’99 organized tutorials in mobile data and transaction management, electronic data security, internet protocol, web warehouse design, etc. About 500 participants attended these previous meetings.

                        Second Call for Papers
Authors are requested to submit original unpublished manuscripts of current research in all areas of Information Technology. Topics of interest include but are not limited to the following:
    •  Web Technology
    • Multi-site Software Development
    • Software-based communication system
    • Strategic Impact of Information Technology
    • Parallel and Distributed Computing
    • Multi-Agent Systems
    • Networking and Ubiquitous Computing
    • Soft Computing
    • E-Commerce and Security
    • Graphics and Human-Computer Interactions
    • Design and Architecture of IT Chips
    • Software Fault Tolerance and Reliability
    • QoS (Quality of Service) in Application Software
    • Data Mining and Warehousing
Send four copies of full manuscript including drawing & illustrations to either of the Program Chairs. The size of each submission is limited to 6000 words of text. Authors are encouraged to submit manuscript electronically (in form of readble level 2 postscript file). The submission must include header information containing Name of authors, abstract, postal address, email and telephone and fax numbers. But hard copy submission is also acceptable. All manuscripts will be reviewed. Deadline for receipt of manuscript is extended to 15th July, 2000. Notification for review result will be mailed to the authors by 18th August, 2000. Camera ready copies due by  6th October, 2000. Proceeding will be available during the conference.

About Bhubaneswar:      (http://www.orissaindia.com):
Bhubaneswar , capital of the state of Orissa, is located in the eastern part of India between Bengal and Andhra Pradesh. There are convenient air and surface transportation to Bhubaneswar. The areas around Bhubaneswar have several major attractions. The magnificent Sun Temple of Konark (known as Black Pagoda) is internationally renowned. Puri and Konark -- both within 30 miles of Bhubaneswar – have beautiful beaches. Close to Bhubaneswar is Nandankanan Zoological Park, a botanical garden that includes the largest lion and tiger safari in India. The Udayagiri and Khandagiri hills at the outskirt of the city have a network of man-made caves dating from the fourth century B.C. Presently, Bhubaneswar offers a great promise to IT industries enthused by several liberalisation policies of local government. Some nationally renowned IT industries have started operating from the city. It is the fastest growing city in the eastern region of India.

Orissa Information Technology Society (OITS): (http://www.oits.org):
OITS is a Society of Computer lovers promoting computer education and usage in Orissa. It is mainly a body of educationists and researchers who wish to help in fast growth of computers, having also a strong component of Computer Scientists outside the state and the country. It organised the International Conferences on Information Technology in December 1998 and December 1999, the Proceedings of which were published by Tata Mc.Graw Hill. Last years Proceedings was sponsored by NALCO, Bhubaneswar.

Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT):    (http://www.kiit.ac.in):
KIIT is a leading educational Institution in Bhubaneswar offering B.E in Computer Science, in Information Technology, and, also a course in Master in Computer Science and Applications. It sponsored the publication of the Conference Proceedings of  1998.
